## Design Context

### Users
UF students in Gainesville — finding this through word of mouth, Instagram, their property manager. They're on their phones, they're skeptical, they want to feel like they're booking someone they can trust, not a faceless company. They're probably in an apartment with a bad pet stain or move-out stress.

### Brand Personality
Local, warm, real. Two actual brothers — not a franchise, not a call center. Think: a friend who happens to be really good at cleaning carpets and will give you a straight answer.
3 words: **grounded, confident, human**

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark hunter green base — established and liked by client. Keep it. But push it further:
- Much bolder, larger typography — headlines should feel heavy and confident
- More breathing room — generous whitespace, nothing crammed
- Interactive and alive — every card, button, and element should react visibly to hover
- Clean like LeaseMe but warmer — not clinical, not corporate
- Fonts: Bricolage Grotesque (display/headings) + Manrope (body) — avoid the banned list

### Design Principles
1. **Make it feel real** — Two actual people built this. Every design decision should feel human, not templated.
2. **Bold over subtle** — Big headlines, strong hierarchy. Students respond to confidence.
3. **Interact with everything** — Hover states, smooth transitions, cards that feel alive.
4. **Space is your friend** — More room, less clutter. LeaseMe's secret is breathing room.
5. **Green is the identity** — Dark hunter green is the brand. Own it, don't apologize for it.
